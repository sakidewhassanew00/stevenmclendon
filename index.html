Choosing the Right GPS Tracker with Kill Switch: Features and Considerations

So, you've decided it's time to get a GPS tracker for your vehicle. A smart move considering how many cars are stolen each year. But not just any GPS tracker will do. You want one with a kill switch feature that can disable your vehicle remotely in case of theft. That way the thieves can't get far and you have a better chance of getting your ride back in one piece. But with so many options out there, how do you choose the right GPS tracker with kill switch for your needs? You'll want to consider things like accuracy, coverage, battery life, and extra features. This guide will walk you through the key features and considerations to help you find a quality GPS tracker with kill switch that gives you peace of mind if your vehicle goes missing.

Why Use a GPS Tracker With Kill Switch?

A GPS tracker with kill switch allows you to remotely disable the device in case it gets lost or stolen. This gives you peace of mind that your tracker can’t be used to monitor you without your consent.

Say you have a tracker on your vehicle for security and location monitoring. If your car is stolen, a kill switch means you can instantly disable the tracker so thieves can’t use it to monitor your driving habits or track your daily routine.

Kill switches are also useful if you lend your tracker to someone else temporarily. You can disable it until you get it back to ensure no one else is accessing the data or using it to keep tabs on you.

Some trackers with kill switches allow you to set up automatic disable alerts. For example, if the device moves outside a set geofence area or at certain times of day, it will automatically disable itself. This is an easy way to keep tabs on your tracker and ensure it’s only operating when and where it’s supposed to be.

Of course, a kill switch is only useful if you have a way to quickly and easily disable the tracker from a distance. Look for a device with an app or online portal that makes disabling your tracker secure but straightforward. The ability to not just disable but also re-enable your tracker once it’s recovered is important too.

With so much sensitive data potentially accessible through a GPS tracker, a kill switch gives you control and peace of mind. For many, the added security and privacy it provides is well worth the investment.

Key Features to Look for in a GPS Tracker

When choosing a GPS tracker, there are a few key features you'll want to consider:
Real-Time Tracking Look for a tracker that offers real-time location updates so you know where your vehicle is at all times. Many trackers update every 30 seconds to 2 minutes so you get near instant notifications. For the best tracking, choose a device with both GPS and cellular connectivity.
Long Battery Life The longer the battery life the better, especially if you plan to use the tracker for extended tracking. Most trackers last 2-4 weeks on a single charge, but some can last up to 2-3 months. Battery life will depend on the frequency of location updates, so less frequent updates typically mean longer battery life.
Geofencing Geofencing allows you to set up virtual boundaries for your vehicle. If the vehicle moves outside the geofenced area, you'll receive an alert. Geofencing is great for theft prevention and monitoring unauthorized use. Many trackers allow you to set multiple geofences with different alerts for each zone.
Alert Types Look for a tracker that offers a variety of alert types like speeding, low battery, entering/exiting a geofence, start/stop moving, etc. The more alert types the better so you can fully monitor your vehicle's activity and location.
Kill Switch A kill switch allows you to remotely disable your vehicle's starter or fuel pump to prevent unauthorized use or theft. Kill switch capability is a must-have feature for any GPS tracker used for security and theft prevention. With the right features like real-time tracking, geofencing alerts, and a kill switch, a GPS tracker can give you peace of mind about the location and security of your vehicle. Compare different trackers to find one with all the features that are most important to you.

Top 5 GPS Trackers with Kill Switch

When choosing a GPS tracker with kill switch, there are several top models to consider based on your needs and budget. The key features to evaluate are:

Battery Life

For most people, longer battery life is better. Some trackers can last 2-4 weeks on a single charge, while others may only last a couple of days. If you need constant real-time tracking, choose a model with a built-in battery that can be recharged or one that allows you to connect an external power source.

Accuracy

The accuracy of the location data is critical. Look for a model with GPS and GLONASS for the most precise location information. Some trackers can pinpoint a location within just a few feet, while others may only provide a general area of a few city blocks.

Size

Do you need a compact, lightweight model or is a larger unit with more features OK? The smallest trackers are about the size of a matchbox and can easily slip into a pocket or bag. Larger units are still portable but may be too bulky for some uses.

Additional Features

Consider what extra features would be useful like geofencing alerts, emergency SOS buttons, voice communication, crash detection or ignition lock. Some trackers also allow you to set speed limit alerts or get reports on driving behavior. Choose features that match how you intend to use the device.

Cost

Prices for GPS trackers with kill switch currently range from around $25 up to $200 or more for a high-end model with lots of advanced features. You can often find good, basic trackers for under $50 that will suit most needs. Consider how often and intensely you need to track to determine an appropriate budget.

By evaluating these factors, you can choose a GPS tracker with kill switch that has the right mix of must-have features at a price you can afford. The top models offer an excellent combination of compact size, long battery life, precise location accuracy and useful extras to give you peace of mind about the location of your vehicles, assets or loved ones.

Testing Coverage and Reliability

Testing the coverage and reliability of a GPS tracker is one of the most important steps before making your final purchase. You want to be confident that the device can do its main job—track locations accurately.

Coverage Area

The coverage area refers to the geographic locations where the GPS device is able to pick up signals from satellites and cellular networks to pinpoint its position. If you plan to use the tracker primarily in remote areas, look for a model that offers nationwide coverage. For use mainly in cities and suburbs, most standard options should work fine. Some brands may list the specific networks they use for connectivity, such as Verizon, AT&T or T-Mobile. Check what networks have the best coverage in your area. You can also contact customer support to inquire about coverage maps for their devices. They should be able to provide an estimate of the percentage of time you can expect a strong, accurate signal.

Reliability

A reliable GPS tracker is one that provides consistent, precise location information when you need it. Look for reviews that speak to the dependability and accuracy of the readings. Things like how often locations are updated, if there are any delays in receiving pings or if the device has ever failed to update for a period of time. Some trackers allow you to set the frequency of location updates, from real-time monitoring to updates every few minutes or hours. More frequent updates will give you better reliability and peace of mind. However, it may reduce the battery life.

Comparing Monthly Fees and Contracts

When choosing a GPS tracker, one of the biggest factors to consider is the monthly service fee and contract. These can vary greatly between devices and providers, so you’ll want to compare carefully.

Monthly Fees

Most GPS trackers charge an ongoing monthly fee to access the tracking platform and cellular connectivity. Fees typically range from $5 to $30 per month. The more basic, budget-friendly trackers will be on the lower end of that scale, while trackers with extra features like temperature monitoring or crash detection will be higher. Some companies offer discounts if you pay annually instead of month-to-month.

Contracts

Some GPS companies require you to sign a 1- or 2-year service contract to get their tracker, while others are contract-free. If you want flexibility, look for trackers with no long-term contract. That way you can cancel at any time if you’re unsatisfied with the service or find a better deal. However, you may be able to get a lower monthly rate by agreeing to a 12- or 24-month contract. Just be sure you understand the terms, like any penalties for early termination.

When evaluating costs, also ask about:

• Activation or setup fees: Some companies charge an initial one-time fee to activate your new GPS tracker.
• Hardware costs: If you cancel service, you'll typically own the tracker hardware. Factor in the upfront cost of the device in your comparisons.
• International coverage: If you need tracking in Mexico, Canada or overseas, fees may be higher. Check if coverage is included or requires an add-on plan.

By comparing the monthly fees, contracts, and other costs across different GPS companies and trackers, you can find an affordable option that fits your needs and budget. The upfront investment in time will give you peace of mind that you're getting the best value for your money.

Conclusion

At the end of the day, you need to find a GPS tracker that gives you peace of mind while also fitting your budget. Do some research on the different models out there and think about what features are most important to you. Whether you want real-time tracking, geo-fencing alerts, or a device with a long battery life, there are good options for every need. Once you've found some strong contenders, compare prices from different retailers to get the best deal. And don't forget that using a GPS tracker with a kill switch can help give you an extra layer of security in case your device ever gets lost or stolen. With the right device, you'll be able to keep tabs on the things that matter most.
